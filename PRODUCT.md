# Product

## Register

product

## Users

French-speaking professionals and businesses who need to create, manage, and analyze dynamic QR codes at scale. They are marketers, operations managers, and agency owners who rely on QR codes as part of their production workflow — not casual users making a single QR code.

Their context: working in a browser, often with multiple tabs open, switching between dashboard views. They expect speed, reliability, and data they can trust. They are not looking for a "fun" experience; they need a tool that respects their time and never surprises them.

## Product Purpose

QR Studio is a SaaS platform for generating, managing, and tracking dynamic QR codes. Dynamic QR codes let users change the destination URL after printing, making them essential for production-grade campaigns. The product exists to give professionals full control over their QR code lifecycle — creation, targeting, monitoring, and optimization — in one focused workspace.

Success means users trust QR Studio enough to print thousands of codes, knowing they can update destinations, pause campaigns, and access analytics without friction.

## Brand Personality

fiable · moderne · efficace

Reliable — the interface never surprises, data is always accurate, the tool works when you need it.
Modern — clean, current, deliberate. Not trendy, but not dated.
Efficient — every screen gets to the point. Dense information, minimal hand-holding, keyboard-friendly.

The tone should inspire mastery and clarity, not flashy innovation. Think tool, not toy.

## Anti-references

- Generic blue/white B2B SaaS (the "corporate startup" look). No navy-and-white, no rounded-corner-everything, no hero-metric templates.
- Cluttered dashboards with excessive cards, gradients, giant stats, or decorative animations.
- Consumer-focused QR code generators (playful, gamified, over-branded).
- Side-stripe borders, gradient text, glassmorphism — these are always wrong here.

Reference energy: **Linear, Raycast, Vercel**. Sober, dense, precise, productivity-oriented. Color is used sparingly and meaningfully. Typography and spacing do the heavy lifting.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every element communicates its purpose instantly. Decorative flourishes that don't serve the task are removed without mercy.

2. **Expert confidence.** The interface trusts the user. Dense information layouts, keyboard navigation, minimal friction. No hand-holding wizards for experienced users.

3. **Precision tooling.** QR codes are about exactness. The UI reflects that: tight spacing, exact alignment, measurable outcomes. A tool that feels engineered, not painted.

4. **Subtle identity.** Brand lives in craft, not decoration. Color is reserved for functional roles (data, states, interaction). The interface is quiet so the data can speak.

5. **Accessibility as quality.** WCAG 2.1 AA is a design constraint that produces better interfaces for everyone. High contrast, full keyboard coverage, never color-only states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG 2.1 AA
- High contrast in both light and dark modes
- Full keyboard navigation throughout
- Screen reader compatible (ARIA labels, semantic HTML, focus management)
- Critical states (errors, status changes, pauses) never communicated by color alone — always accompanied by text, icons, or patterns
- Analytics tables, data visualizations, and QR code previews must remain legible at all zoom levels
- Support for reduced motion preferences
